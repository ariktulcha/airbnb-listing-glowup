## Angle

The title should quickly communicate the listing’s strongest guest-relevant advantage. Generic adjectives waste space.

## Draft introduction

“Cozy apartment.” “Beautiful home.” “Nice studio.” These phrases are common, but they do not tell guests why they should choose your place over a similar one nearby.

A stronger Airbnb title is specific. It uses the limited title space to highlight the feature, location, layout, or experience that matters most to the target guest.

## Title formula

**[Specific property type or layout] + [strongest differentiator] + [guest-relevant feature]**

Examples:
- “Sunny 1BR Near Retiro Park + Balcony”
- “Design Loft with Acropolis View + Workspace”
- “Family 3BR with Pool, Parking + Full Kitchen”
- “Quiet Studio Steps to Metro + Fast Wi-Fi”
- “Beachfront Condo with Ocean View + King Bed”

## What to avoid

- Generic adjectives: beautiful, cozy, nice, amazing.
- Keyword stuffing.
- ALL CAPS.
- Claims that are not obvious or defensible.
- Mentioning every amenity instead of the strongest one.

## Rewrite examples

### Example 1

Weak: “Cozy Apartment in City Center”  
Better: “Bright 1BR Walkable to Old Town + Balcony”

### Example 2

Weak: “Luxury Stay With Everything You Need”  
Better: “Modern 2BR with Rooftop Pool + Free Parking”

### Example 3

Weak: “Nice Studio Near Attractions”  
Better: “Quiet Studio by Metro + Dedicated Workspace”

### Example 4

Weak: “Beautiful Home for Your Vacation”  
Better: “Family Home with Garden, Grill + 3 Bedrooms”

## Choosing the right title angle

Ask what the guest is really shopping for:
- Location convenience?
- Sleep quality?
- Group comfort?
- Design/aesthetic?
- Outdoor space?
- Work setup?
- Family practicality?
- View or unique feature?
