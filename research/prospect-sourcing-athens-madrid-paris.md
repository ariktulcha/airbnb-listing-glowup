# Prospect Sourcing Routes — Athens, Madrid, Paris

*Created: 2026-07-03*  
*Purpose: practical customer/prospect sourcing plan for validating the Airbnb Listing Glow-Up service with reachable hosts/operators. This is not a market-size report.*

## Executive takeaways

1. **Start with Athens as the fastest validation city.** It has many independent-looking central apartments, a multilingual tourism market, and likely better contactability through direct sites, Google Business Profiles, Booking.com, Vrbo, Instagram, and small local operators.
2. **Madrid is the best second test for volume.** The city has enough central apartment inventory and many small professional operators, but contact routes will skew toward property managers and Spanish-language outreach.
3. **Paris should be treated as higher-value but slower.** The economics are attractive, but hosts/operators are more professionalized, regulated, and privacy-sensitive. The best Paris route is through conciergerie/property-management companies and direct websites, not anonymous individual Airbnb hosts.
4. **Do not source through Airbnb guest messaging.** Use Airbnb only as a discovery and qualification signal. Outreach should happen only via independently public business/contact channels.
5. **The first 50 prospects should be a contactability test, not a giant lead list.** A prospect is useful only if it has (a) a fixable presentation gap, (b) enough ADR/reviews to justify a $300 one-time service, and (c) a legitimate non-Airbnb contact path.

## Working definition of a qualified prospect

A qualified prospect is one of:

- **Independent host with public contact route:** same property name appears on Booking.com/Vrbo/direct site/social profile/Google Business Profile.
- **Small property manager/operator:** 5–80 listings, public website/contact form/email/LinkedIn/Instagram, listings look inconsistent enough to need a lightweight listing upgrade.
- **High-value apartment with weak presentation:** strong location/reviews/price, but cover photo, title, opening paragraph, photo order, or English copy is visibly below the underlying property quality.

Disqualify leads when the only route is Airbnb messaging, the listing is already professionally presented, the property quality itself is weak, or the host/operator appears too large and brand-controlled for a $300 tactical offer.

## City-by-city plan

### Athens

**Hypothesis:** best first city for rapid validation.

**Reachable host segments**

- Independent central apartment hosts around Plaka, Koukaki, Monastiraki, Psyrri, Syntagma, Neos Kosmos, Kolonaki.
- Small operators managing furnished apartments and aparthotel-style units.
- High-review apartments with generic English titles or underwhelming cover photos.
- Family/couple apartments where the property is good but the listing does not communicate location, balcony/view, work setup, or transport clearly.

**Best sourcing routes**

1. Airbnb manual discovery → exact title/name Google search.
2. Google Maps: local STR managers, serviced apartments, vacation-rental managers.
3. Booking.com cross-listing by exact property name.
4. Instagram property pages and small operator profiles.
5. Vrbo duplicate search for larger apartments/homes.

**High-intent search queries**

- `Athens Airbnb property management contact`
- `Athens vacation rental management apartment contact`
- `site:booking.com Athens "apartment" "Acropolis" "host"`
- `site:vrbo.com Athens "Acropolis" "apartment"`
- `"Athens" "short term rental" "property management"`
- `"Athens" "serviced apartments" "contact"`
- Greek-language variants: `διαχείριση Airbnb Αθήνα`, `διαχείριση βραχυχρόνιας μίσθωσης Αθήνα`, `ενοικιαζόμενα διαμερίσματα Αθήνα contact`

**What to look for**

- Good property/reviews but weak English positioning.
- Strong location not reflected in title/first photo.
- Photo order starts with decor/bed instead of strongest room/view/location asset.
- Listings that appear on Booking.com with a brand/property name searchable elsewhere.

**Contactability expectation:** highest of the three cities. Benchmark: if fewer than 15 of the first 50 Athens candidates have a non-Airbnb contact path, the workflow is too Airbnb-dependent and should shift to operator directories/maps.

### Madrid

**Hypothesis:** strong lead volume, moderate contactability, more operator-heavy than Athens.

**Reachable host segments**

- Central 1–2 bedroom apartments in Centro, Malasaña, Chueca, La Latina, Lavapiés, Salamanca, Retiro.
- Small Spanish property managers with mixed-quality listing portfolios.
- Listings with generic titles such as “Studio,” “Modern studio,” “Deluxe apartment,” where the property likely has a stronger differentiator.
- Operators whose English copy is literal/flat while photos are adequate.

**Best sourcing routes**

1. Google Maps and Google Search in Spanish for `gestión apartamentos turísticos`.
2. Airbnb discovery → exact title/property name search.
3. Booking.com cross-listing for aparthotel/managed apartment names.
4. LinkedIn search for founders/operators of small STR management companies.
5. Instagram/Facebook for boutique apartment brands.

**High-intent search queries**

- `Madrid apartamentos turísticos gestión contacto`
- `Madrid gestión Airbnb propietarios contacto`
- `Madrid short term rental property manager contact`
- `site:booking.com Madrid "apartment" "Plaza Mayor"`
- `site:vrbo.com Madrid "Gran Via" apartment`
- `"Madrid" "apartamentos turísticos" "contacto"`
- `"Madrid" "alquiler turístico" "gestión integral"`
- `LinkedIn Madrid short term rental property manager`

**What to look for**

- High review counts but weak titles/opening copy.
- Professional operators with inconsistent listing quality across properties.
- Listings where Spanish copy is acceptable but English copy is not conversion-focused.
- Properties in expensive neighborhoods that can justify a $300 service.

**Contactability expectation:** medium. Expect more B2B operator outreach than independent host outreach. Spanish-language outreach will likely outperform English-first outreach.

### Paris

**Hypothesis:** highest willingness-to-pay but hardest cold sourcing.

**Reachable host segments**

- Conciergerie/property-management companies with public sites and small-to-mid portfolios.
- Boutique aparthotel / serviced-apartment operators with inconsistent OTA presentation.
- Independent-looking high-ADR apartments where a public direct site/social page exists.
- Listings in Le Marais, Latin Quarter, Montmartre, Bastille, Saint-Germain, Canal Saint-Martin, Eiffel/Trocadéro where presentation is visibly below price level.

**Best sourcing routes**

1. Google Maps: `Paris conciergerie Airbnb`, `gestion location courte durée Paris`.
2. Direct property manager/conciergerie websites and contact forms.
3. LinkedIn operator/founder outreach.
4. Booking.com cross-listing where property name/operator can be searched externally.
5. Airbnb discovery only as a secondary qualification step.

**High-intent search queries**

- `Paris conciergerie Airbnb contact`
- `gestion location courte durée Paris contact`
- `Paris property management Airbnb owners`
- `site:booking.com Paris "apartment" "Le Marais"`
- `site:vrbo.com Paris "Le Marais" apartment`
- `"Paris" "conciergerie" "Airbnb" "contact"`
- `"location courte durée" "Paris" "gestion"`
- `LinkedIn Paris conciergerie Airbnb founder`

**What to look for**

- Operator websites whose own presentation is polished but individual OTA listings are uneven.
- Premium listings with weak first-photo choice or generic titles.
- Multilingual copy gaps: French-first copy that does not sell well to international guests.
- Portfolio operators where a single paid upgrade can become a multi-listing pilot.

**Contactability expectation:** lower for individual hosts, higher for operators. Use a business-development tone, not a host-roast tone.

## OTA cross-listing tactic without violating Airbnb norms

Use platforms as public research signals, not as places to impersonate a guest.

1. Start from a manually reviewed Airbnb listing only to identify a presentation gap.
2. Copy distinctive non-personal signals: property title, unique apartment name, neighborhood, visible operator/brand name if present.
3. Search exact-match combinations on Google:
   - `"[exact listing title]" "[city]"`
   - `"[property name]" "Booking.com"`
   - `"[property name]" "Vrbo"`
   - `"[host/operator name]" "[city]" "contact"`
4. If a Booking.com/Vrbo duplicate is found, use it to confirm property identity and extract a stable public property/operator name.
5. Search that property/operator name for a direct website, Google Business Profile, Instagram, Facebook, or LinkedIn page.
6. Outreach only through public business channels: direct website form, listed email, listed phone/WhatsApp if business-facing, LinkedIn, or public social profile.
7. In the CRM, store only the minimal facts needed: source URL, contact route, city, listing score, reason for fit, outreach status. Do not download/rehost property images unless making a private watermarked preview for an outreach test.

## Contactability and compliance risks

| Risk | Practical rule |
|---|---|
| Airbnb platform rules / anti-scraping / commercial use concerns | Use manual discovery and minimal notes. Do not automate Airbnb scraping. Do not message hosts as a fake guest. Review Airbnb terms/help pages before scaling. |
| Fake guest / pretexting risk | Never ask availability questions or pretend to book. The offer is a B2B listing-presentation service. |
| Spam/GDPR/ePrivacy risk | Contact only via public business channels where the message is relevant to the business activity. Keep it one-to-one and personalized. Include a simple opt-out/no-follow-up note. |
| Photo/copyright sensitivity | Use watermarked previews privately. Do not publish before/after examples from real listings without permission. Do not imply ownership of host photos. |
| Misleading edits | Pitch professionalized presentation only: crop, brightness, color, order, title/copy, captions, reel. No fake amenities, fake views, fake room size, or hidden defects. |
| Reputation risk | Avoid public “roast” framing. Lead with “your property appears stronger than the listing communicates.” |
| Contact-route mismatch | If the public contact appears to belong to a third-party agency, address it as an operator/agency pitch, not as if they personally own the property. |

## First 50-prospect research workflow

### Target mix

For the first 50 prospects, do not split evenly by city. Use the mix below to learn fastest:

- **Athens: 22 prospects** — highest probability of independent reachable hosts.
- **Madrid: 16 prospects** — strong volume and B2B operator path.
- **Paris: 12 prospects** — higher value, but emphasize operators/conciergeries.

Within those 50:

- 25 from Airbnb manual discovery + cross-search.
- 12 from Google Maps/business profiles/property-manager search.
- 8 from Booking.com/Vrbo cross-listing discovery.
- 5 from LinkedIn/Instagram/operator social search.

### Data columns to add to the lead sheet

Use or extend `templates/lead-scoring.csv` with these operational columns:

- `city`
- `source_route` — Airbnb cross-search / Google Maps / Booking / Vrbo / LinkedIn / Instagram / direct operator site
- `listing_url`
- `operator_or_host_name`
- `public_contact_url`
- `contact_channel` — form / email / Instagram / LinkedIn / phone / WhatsApp / none
- `contactability_score_0_20`
- `presentation_gap_score_0_30`
- `economic_fit_score_0_20`
- `trust_activity_score_0_15`
- `personalization_angle`
- `first_message_version`
- `status`
- `do_not_contact_reason`

### Step-by-step process

1. **Create a raw pool of 120 candidates** in 4–6 hours:
   - Athens 45, Madrid 40, Paris 35.
   - Do not deeply research each one yet; capture URL/name/neighborhood/review count/visible reason for gap.
2. **Fast disqualify to 70 candidates:** remove listings with no reviews, weak property quality, already-excellent presentation, too-low price, or only anonymous Airbnb contact.
3. **Contactability pass:** spend up to 5 minutes per candidate looking for a legitimate public contact route. If none, mark `contactability_failed` and move on.
4. **Score the best 50:** combine contactability + presentation gap + economic fit. A high-gap listing without contact route is not a priority.
5. **Pick top 15 for mini-previews:** 7 Athens, 5 Madrid, 3 Paris. Do not produce previews for all 50.
6. **Send only 5 initial outreach messages first:** 3 Athens, 1 Madrid, 1 Paris. Test deliverability and tone before scaling.
7. **Decision checkpoint after 5–10 sends:**
   - If replies ask for details/pricing: create more previews in that city.
   - If no replies but messages are likely delivered: change subject/opening and use permission-first framing.
   - If contactability is poor: shift sourcing route from Airbnb cross-search to Google Maps/operator directories.
   - If pushback is price: test a $149 diagnostic creditable toward $300 full upgrade.

### Time box

| Task | Time |
|---|---:|

| Contactability pass | 5–6 hours |
| Score first 50 | 2 hours |

## Outreach positioning by segment

### Independent host

Use permission-first, short, and respectful:

> I came across your [city/neighborhood] apartment while researching strong properties with fixable listing-presentation gaps. The property itself looks solid; I think the cover photo/title/first paragraph may be underselling it. I made a small private watermarked preview showing the direction. Want me to send it?

### Small property manager

Use portfolio efficiency angle:

> I help short-term-rental operators quickly upgrade underperforming listing presentation: photo order, cover-photo direction, titles, descriptions, captions, and a short reel. I noticed a few [city] listings where the property looks stronger than the OTA presentation. Would it be useful if I sent one private watermarked example?

### Paris / professional operator

Use less casual, more operational wording:

> I work on OTA listing presentation for short-term-rental operators: improving clarity, photo sequencing, multilingual copy, and guest-facing positioning without changing the property or making misleading edits. I found one listing in your portfolio where a small presentation refresh may be worthwhile. May I send a private example?

## City prioritization after first 50

Rank cities using this score after the first 50:

`city_score = qualified_70_plus + reachable_contacts + positive_replies - contactability_failures - compliance_concerns`

Suggested decision rules:

- **Double down on Athens** if 30%+ have valid public contact and at least 8/22 score 70+.
- **Expand Madrid** if operator reply rate is better than independent-host reply rate, even if listing gaps are less obvious.
- **Keep Paris narrow** unless operator contactability is strong; do not burn time on anonymous individual hosts.
