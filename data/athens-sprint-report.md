# Athens cold-outreach validation sprint report

## CONTACTABILITY HIT-RATE

**42/42 assessed properties had a real reachable non-Airbnb contact route.**

## GO / NO-GO

**GO, but with an agency-heavy caveat.** Reachable Athens listings absolutely exist when the funnel is inverted through operator sites instead of Airbnb. The bottleneck was not contact discovery; it was finding independent individual hosts. **37/42 contacts were agencies/operators**, so the pitch should be framed as a free micro-audit for one listing and an optional per-listing upgrade, not as a personal host favor. Olala inventory had easy contactability but weak economics/low ADR; UPSTREET supplied the strongest reachable central-Athens candidates.

## Sourcing notes

- Scope: Athens only; no website edits; no new strategy docs; no outreach sent.

- Sources used: public property/operator pages with visible contact routes. No Airbnb search scraping.

- Qualified rule applied: `score>=70` and `contactable=yes`.

- Qualified leads: **19/42**.

## Channel bottleneck / quality read

- **UPSTREET:** best channel for central neighborhoods and ADR fit. Contact is easy, but it is an agency/operator inbox, not an individual host. Presentation gaps are mostly title/story/photo-order gaps, not catastrophic bad photography.

- **The Athenians:** direct contact found and central Syntagma product, but the public pages are more branded/portfolio-style; not enough ADR/review proof captured to qualify top-five.

- **Olala Homes:** easiest to source at volume and contactable, but many Athens units were low ADR (€35–€90) and/or outside the requested neighborhoods, so they are weak fits for a $300 upgrade.

- **Independent hosts:** not enough verified individual-host contact routes in this pass. If the next sprint requires individual hosts, the bottleneck is discovering public non-Airbnb contact pages, not assessing listing quality.
