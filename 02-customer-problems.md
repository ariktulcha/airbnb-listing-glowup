# Customer Problems

## Main customer

Small Airbnb / short-term-rental hosts with 1–5 listings who are not strong in digital marketing, design, photography, SEO, copywriting, or revenue optimization.

Best early ICP:

- 1–5 properties
- ADR roughly $100–$300/night
- listing has poor photos/copy but property looks fixable
- active listing with reviews
- rating above ~4.6
- host is reachable outside Airbnb
- host is commercially motivated

Possible niche wedge:

> Israeli / Hebrew-speaking owners with apartments in Europe.

## Problems to solve

### 1. Weak listing presentation
Many hosts have decent apartments but poor cover photo, lighting, photo order, composition, title, description, captions, and amenity emphasis.

### 2. Low conversion
They may get views but not bookings because guests do not immediately understand why they should choose this property.

### 3. Poor positioning
The listing does not clearly say who it is for: couples, families, digital nomads, business travelers, budget travelers, or luxury weekenders.

### 4. Bad expectation-setting
Bad copy and missing details can create disappointed guests and weaker reviews.

### 5. Host does not understand competitors
Hosts rarely know what nearby competitors show first, how competitors describe the area, what amenities competitors highlight, whether their price feels premium or budget, or whether their listing looks weaker than its price.

### 6. Review insights are unused
Reviews contain useful sales copy and operational warnings: repeated compliments, repeated complaints, hidden selling points, guest language, and expectation mismatches.

### 7. No digital confidence
Many hosts are not digital marketers. They need someone to close the loop for them.

## Strong sales insight

The pitch should not be “AI.” The host cares about more bookings, better presentation, fewer lost clicks, stronger guest trust, and a one-time done-for-you fix.

## Key selling sentence

> Your property may not be the problem. Your listing presentation may be costing you bookings.
