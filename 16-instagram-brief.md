Link-in-bio: free "Listing Score" lead magnet form (already exists in the project) → funnels to the $300 offer.

## Profile positioning

- Profile photo: the close-crop portrait shot (consistent face, casual-smart, warm neutral tone).
- Name field: "Ryan Ashford — Airbnb Listings" (searchable keyword in the name field itself).
- First 3 grid posts should each answer one question at a glance: who I am, what I fix, proof it works.

## Visual direction

- Feed grid: alternate between (1) Ryan in work/behind-the-scenes shots, (2) before/after listing photo comparisons, (3) short educational text-card graphics (dark background, one bold line, brand accent color).
- Consistent warm-neutral, slightly desaturated grade across every photo of Ryan.
- Typography for text cards: clean sans-serif, high contrast, max 12 words per card.
- No stock photography. No generic "Airbnb interior" images that aren't tied to a real (or realistically rendered) before/after example.

## Highlights structure

1. **Start Here** — who Ryan is, what the service does, link to offer
2. **Before/After** — real transformation previews (rotates as new client work lands)
3. **Listing Fixes** — quick tips (cover photo, title, captions, order)
4. **Pricing** — the $300 package breakdown, what's included
5. **FAQ** — how it works, turnaround time, guarantee
6. **Behind the Work** — travel/BTS content, humanizes the account

## First 10 content ideas

1. Carousel: "3 things I look at in the first 5 seconds of any Airbnb listing" (educational, establishes authority)
2. Before/after carousel: a real or sample listing cover-photo swap
3. Reel: quick voiceover breakdown of a weak listing's photo order
4. Text-card post: "Your Airbnb may not need a better property. It may need a better presentation."
5. Carousel: "5 photos every Airbnb listing needs in this exact order"
6. Behind-the-scenes photo: Ryan reviewing a listing on his laptop, caption about the process
7. Carousel: "3 title mistakes that make guests scroll past your listing"
8. Reel: "This cover photo is losing bookings" quick teardown
9. Post: introduce the $300 Glow-Up package, plain breakdown of what's included
10. Carousel: "What I'd fix first in a weak Airbnb listing" — generic teardown template hosts can self-apply

## Posting cadence (launch phase)
3-4 posts/week, mix of feed posts + Reels + Stories. Reels get priority for reach; feed posts build the credibility library people check when they land on the profile from outreach.
