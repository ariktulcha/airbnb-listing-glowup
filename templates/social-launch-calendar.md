### Post 2 — educational

The first five Airbnb photos have jobs:

1. Cover photo: stop the scroll
2. Bedroom / sleep proof: can I rest here?
3. Kitchen / practical proof: can I live here?
4. Bathroom / cleanliness proof: can I trust it?
5. Location/lifestyle proof: why this stay, not another?

Most weak listings do not have bad properties. They have random photo order.

CTA: I made a free listing scorecard for this exact problem.

### Post 3 — title before/after

Weak Airbnb title:
“Beautiful apartment in city center”

Better direction:
“Bright balcony stay near cafés + metro”

Why it works:
- specific guest benefit
- location trigger
- visual promise
- avoids generic adjectives

The goal is not clever copy. The goal is faster guest understanding.

### Post 4 — trust / ethics

A listing glow-up should never make the property look like something it is not.

Good edits:
- brighter photos
- better crop
- cleaner order
- clearer title
- captions that explain real amenities

Bad edits:
- fake views
- fake room size
- fake luxury language
- fake reviews
- revenue guarantees

Trust is the product.
