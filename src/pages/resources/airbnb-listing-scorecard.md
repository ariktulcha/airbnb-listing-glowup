## What this lead magnet helps you decide

The scorecard helps you answer one question: is your listing underperforming because the property is weak, or because the presentation is weak? Many hosts jump to pricing too quickly. The scorecard slows that decision down and forces a structured review of the things guests actually see first.

Use it when your listing gets impressions but not enough clicks, views but not enough bookings, or inquiries from guests who seem confused about the stay. It is also useful before peak season, after a renovation, after changing photography, or before handing a property to a manager.

## The scoring sections

| Section | Points | What it checks |
|---|---:|---|
| Cover photo | 20 | Does the first image create a reason to click? |
| First five photos | 20 | Does the sequence explain the stay quickly? |
| Photo quality and honesty | 15 | Are images bright, straight, useful, and accurate? |
| Title and opening copy | 15 | Does the listing name a specific guest benefit? |
| Captions and details | 10 | Are bed sizes, room use, views, and limitations clear? |
| Trust signals | 10 | Does the listing reduce uncertainty before booking? |
| Competitor fit | 10 | Does the listing look competitive beside nearby options? |

## How to use it in 15 minutes

Open your listing in one tab and three nearby competitor listings in other tabs. Do not start editing yet. First, score honestly. If you are the owner, try to judge like a guest who has no emotional connection to the property.

Give yourself a number for each section. Write one sentence explaining why you gave that score. Then create a top-five action list. The goal is not perfection. The goal is to identify the most expensive presentation leaks.

## What a low score usually means

A low score does not mean the property is bad. It often means the listing is forcing guests to do too much work. The cover may be dark. The title may be generic. The first five photos may hide the best feature. Captions may be empty. The description may list amenities but fail to explain the stay.

Those issues are fixable. More importantly, they are often cheaper to fix than discounting for weeks.

## What happens after the scorecard

If your score is above 80, you may only need small improvements. If it is between 60 and 80, the listing likely has several presentation leaks worth fixing. If it is below 60, the listing probably needs a full re-order, rewrite, and visual diagnosis before you make pricing decisions.

## Important limits

This is not a guarantee of more bookings. It is not Airbnb SEO magic. It is not permission to create misleading photos. The scorecard only helps you judge whether the listing presentation is making the property easier or harder to book.

If the property has operational problems, bad reviews, poor cleanliness, unrealistic pricing, or a weak location for the target guest, presentation alone will not solve that. But if the property is decent and the listing looks worse than it should, this is the right place to start.
